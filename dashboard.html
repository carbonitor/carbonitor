Carbon Offset Projects

Indonesia

The Katingan Restoration and Conservation Project (‘The Katingan Project’) is a significant carbon offset initiative that protects and restores 149,800 hectares of peatland ecosystems. This project plays a crucial role in mitigating global climate change by sequestering carbon dioxide, while also providing sustainable income sources for local communities. Located within the districts of Katingan and Kotawaringin Timur in Central Kalimantan Province, the project safeguards one of Indonesia's largest remaining intact peat swamp forests, making a substantial contribution to carbon offset efforts.

Project Summary
  • Project ID: 1477
  • Proponent: PT. Rimba Makmur Utama (PT. RMU)
  • Project Status: Registered
  • Estimated Annual Emission Reductions: 7,451,846 tCO2e
  • Total Buffer Pool Credits: 3,584,0998 credits
  • Project Type: Agriculture Forestry and Other Land Use
  • Area Covered: 149,800 Hectares
  • Project Validator: SCS Global Services
  • Project Registration Date: 06/04/2020
